Watch it outside

Boston edition

The 10th annual Boston Film Festival is organised by the company Watch it outside and will be held in Boston Public Garden this year from August 5th through 8th, from 6pm to midnight. The Festival is officially recognised by the FIAPF (International Federation of Film Producers Association). The aim of the Festival is to raise awareness and promote international cinema in all its forms as art, entertainment and as an industry, in a spirit of freedom and dialogue. In addition to the sections mentioned in the following paragraphs, the Festival also organises retrospectives and tributes to major figures as a contribution towards a better understanding of the history of cinema.
Considering the situation, this year's festival is organised for smaller group of people, so, if you want to visit us, make sure you have reserved your card on time! We will have a small group of celebrity guests, mostly actors and directors. This year, we offer you a large ammount of movies from different countries (on different languages). Also, there will be a live cast every day. We encourage filmophiles whose health isn't great to stay at their homes and watch movies from their cozy sofas!
Stay tuned and read our everyday news on our webpage.



News


26 years without Milan Mladenovic: Tonight we watch a documentary about him

In honor of the front men of the rock group EKV tonight a documentary film Praia Do Ventu Eternu will be shown at the Watch it outside Boston edition film festival. The film is directed by Djordje Marjanovic and produced by Milan Mladenovic foundation. “Film is composed of private VHS videos that were taken while Milan Mladenovic and Mitar Subotic Suba were in Brazil. The picture is followed by excerpts from Milan`s diary of that time and narration of Svetozar Cvetkovic makes the whole film very touching”, says the report of the foundation.
Film will be shown tonight at 6 P.M. at the main cinema screen.

Looking for love over nicotine and caffeine; Jim Jarmusch talks about Coffee and Cigarettes

Tonight one of the most famous films of Jim Jarmusch will be shown at our festival Watch it outside Boston edition. Coffee and Cigarettes is a playful, complex film and so is its maker, Jim Jarmusch. This film is a compilation of eleven black-and-white shorts the filmmaker made between 1986 and 2003. In the conversation we had with Jarmusch which opened the film festival he states: “I think our lives are made up of little moments that are not necessarily dramatic, and necessarily dramatic, and for some odd reason I`m attracted to those moments.” This movie is certainly a pleasure to watch so make sure you join the festival and give yourself an unforgettable experience.

Premiere of Wes Anderson’s Hotel Chevalier at Watch it outside Boston edition movie festival

On the last day of the festival guests will have a unique opportunity to be the first to watch Anderson`s new short film. The 13-minute film, which serves as a prologue to Darjeeling, is about an American named Jack, holing up in a fancy Paris hotel, who has an unexpected reunion with an estranged girlfriend. Shot two years ago, before screenwriters Anderson, Schwartzman, and Roman Coppola developed the character into one of the three brothers on Darjeeling‘s rail journey across India, Chevalier sees Anderson working in his customary jewel-box/dollhouse mode, but the form and length really suit each other here.

Natalie Portman pays tribute to snubbed female directors at the Watch it outside Boston edition film festival

Natalie Portman and her red carpet attire aren't going to let anyone forget about the lack of female directors nominated for film awards this year. Portman, on hand to present, sported a cape on the red carpet featuring the names of women who were overlooked this year in the directing category, including Greta Gerwig (Little Women), Lorene Scafaria (Hustlers) and Lulu Wang (The Farewell).
Not all heroes wear capes, but Natalie Portman is now one of them, declared Harper`s Bazaar. Bravo, Natalie, added Marie Claire.
